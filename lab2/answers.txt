In the original version, it returned the smallest perfect square greater than or equal to the input value. In my calculate function, I used call-preserved registers such as `%rbx` and `%r12`-`%r15` to store values that need to survive across calls of `f`, including the original `x` and the partial results a, b, c, and d. Since these registers must be preserved for the caller, I saved them with push at the beginning of calculate and restored them with pop before returning.

I also used a non-preserved register `%r10` before calling `f` to demonstrate how caller-saved registers work. Since `f` destroys non-preserved registers, I saved the value of call. This shows that if a value in a non-preserved register is needed after a function call, the caller must save it manually.
